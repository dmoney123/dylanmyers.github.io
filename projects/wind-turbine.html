Wind Turbine Blade Design

Three-part university project at Technical University of Denmark: statistical wind analysis, airfoil design & testing, and optimization for Denmark's Southern Coast.

Project Overview

This wind turbine project was completed at the Technical University of Denmark as part of university coursework, working with a peer. The goal was to design and manufacture a prototype airfoil for a wind turbine optimized for Denmark's Southern Coast conditions.

The project consisted of three parts: statistical wind pattern analysis, engineering design and prototype testing, and computational optimization.

Phase 1: Wind Pattern Analysis

Conducted comprehensive statistical analysis of wind patterns at the DTU Risø Windfarm using Cumulative Distribution Functions (CDF) and Probability Density Functions (PDF); providing critical data on wind speed distributions.

  • • Analyzed historical wind data from DTU Risø Windfarm
  • • Applied statistical methods (CDF, PDF) to characterize wind patterns
  • • Identified optimal operating conditions for blade design

Phase 2: Airfoil Design & Prototype Testing

Applied the engineering design process to create an airfoil.

Design Considerations

  • • S-N fatigue analysis for material selection
  • • Stress-life constraints and safety factors
  • • Reynolds number optimization
  • • Beam theory for structural integrity

Testing & Validation

  • • Prototype manufacturing
  • • Wind tunnel testing

Phase 3: Computational Optimization

Utilized MATLAB software for iterative design optimization.

Optimization Parameters

  • • Chord length variation
  • • Angle of attack optimization
  • • Twist angle distribution
  • • Rotational induction factor

Final Phase

  • • Re-testing optimized design
  • • Performance validation
  • • Competition with peer designs
