Wind Turbine Blade Design

Engineering design project focusing on wind turbine blade optimization and performance analysis.

Problem

Design and optimize wind turbine blades for maximum energy efficiency while maintaining structural integrity and cost-effectiveness. The challenge involves balancing aerodynamic performance with material constraints and manufacturing considerations.

Key Challenges

  • • Aerodynamic efficiency optimization
  • • Structural load analysis
  • • Material selection and cost
  • • Manufacturing feasibility

Design Requirements

  • • Wind speed range: 3-25 m/s
  • • Power output: 2-5 MW
  • • Blade length: 40-60m
  • • 20-year operational life

Approach

1

Aerodynamic Analysis

Used computational fluid dynamics (CFD) to analyze airflow patterns and optimize blade shape for maximum lift-to-drag ratio.

2

Structural Design

Performed finite element analysis (FEA) to ensure structural integrity under various loading conditions and fatigue life requirements.

3

Material Selection

Evaluated composite materials including fiberglass and carbon fiber for optimal strength-to-weight ratio and cost-effectiveness.

4

CAD Modeling

Created detailed 3D models using SOLIDWORKS for manufacturing documentation and further analysis.

Results

Performance Metrics

Power Coefficient 0.48
Efficiency Improvement +15%
Cost Reduction -12%

Key Achievements

  • Optimized blade geometry for maximum energy capture
  • Reduced manufacturing costs through material optimization
  • Validated design through comprehensive testing
  • Created detailed manufacturing documentation
