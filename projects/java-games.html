Java‑Monster

Tower‑defense inspired assignment: monsters advance toward a castle while warriors defend the territory. I built a clean, testable OOP model with composition over inheritance, interfaces for behaviours, and turn‑based sequencing. Combat uses weapon effectiveness tables, rage accumulation and berserk states, and multi‑step attack chains (e.g., piercing or retrieval mechanics).

  • Well‑factored OOP design (composition, interfaces, SOLID)
  • Turn‑based engine with deterministic resolution order
  • Weapon effectiveness matrix; rage/berserk mechanics
  • Edge‑case handling: tile constraints, valid interactions

Block‑Buster

Visual puzzle built on a quad‑tree data structure. Players transform the board to meet a goal (largest colour blob or symmetry) using operations like rotate, reflect, smash, and condense. The Mondrian‑style board highlights recursive structure and makes the rules simple but challenging.

  • Quad‑tree model with recursive operations
  • Goals: max connected blob or horizontal/vertical symmetry
  • Moves: rotate, reflect, smash, condense
  • Immediate scoring feedback after each move
