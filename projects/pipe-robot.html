Pipe Inspecting Robot

Team design of an in‑pipe inspection robot for 300–400 mm pipes. We created a modular chassis, locomotion, and camera integration to navigate T‑junctions, climb vertical sections, and support standard fasteners and motors.

Overview

Course project to design an in‑pipe robot that can traverse 300–400 mm pipes, handle turns at T‑connections, move vertically, and carry a camera for visual inspection. The design emphasizes realistic fastening (bolts/nuts), motor mounting (e.g., NEMA‑8), and modular components.

Key Challenges

  • • Navigating confined, curved pipes and T‑junctions
  • • Maintaining traction in vertical sections
  • • Packaging camera and wiring within a compact chassis
  • • Standard fastener use and realistic assembly

Design Requirements

  • • Pipe diameter: 300–400 mm
  • • Modular chassis with wheel/track locomotion
  • • Motor mounts (e.g., NEMA‑8) and camera bracket
  • • Standard screws, nuts, and bolts for assembly

Approach

1

Conceptual Design

Developed multiple design concepts focusing on modularity and adaptability to different pipe configurations.

2

CAD Modeling

Created detailed 3D models using SOLIDWORKS, including assembly constraints and motion analysis.

3

Mechanism Design

Designed wheel and track systems for optimal traction and maneuverability in various pipe conditions.

4

Integration Planning

Planned sensor and camera mounting points for comprehensive inspection capabilities.

Results

Design Specifications

Adaptability Range 100-500mm
Modular Design 5 Modules
Weight 8.5 kg

Key Features

  • Modular design for easy maintenance and upgrades
  • Adjustable wheel system for different pipe sizes
  • Integrated camera and sensor mounting points
  • Detailed manufacturing documentation
