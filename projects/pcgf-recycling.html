Recycling of PCGF

Summer research project on PCGF recycling, with journal publication coming soon. Includes comprehensive analysis and findings.

View Poster (PDF)

Overview

Investigation into the thermomechanical, structural and rheological properties of pristine vs. recycled thermoplastic sandwich panels, completed at the Structures and Composite Materials Laboratory (SCML), McGill University. We developed and validated a recycling methodology based on shredding and compression moulding, then used a Box–Behnken design of experiments (BBD) to generate a quadratic model for behavioural optimisation.

Materials & Methods

  • Sandwich board laminate: Polymer A skin, Polymer B core, Polymer C adhesive.
  • Recycling: shredding & controlled compression moulding (temperature/pressure/time).
  • Analyses: rheological, structural (optical microscopy), thermal (Tg, degradation), and mechanical (modulus/strength).
  • Design of Experiments: Model built in matlab, varying temperature, pressure, and time; regression to a quadratic response surface for optimization.

Key Results

  • Thermal parity: recycled samples maintain glass transition trends similar to pristine material; degradation near ~410 °C.
  • Optimization: response surface predicts flexural strength maximised at a specific combination of temperature, pressure and time.
  • Mechanical performance: recycled panels achieved meaningful stiffness/strength.
