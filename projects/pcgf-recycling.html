Recycling of PCGF

Summer research project on PCGF recycling, with journal publication coming soon. Includes comprehensive analysis and findings.

Overview

Investigation into the thermomechanical, structural and rheological properties of pristine vs. recycled thermoplastic sandwich panels, completed at the Structures and Composite Materials Laboratory (SCML), McGill University. We developed and validated a recycling methodology based on shredding and compression moulding, then used a Box–Behnken design of experiments (BBD) to generate a quadratic model for behavioural optimisation.

Materials & Methods

  • Sandwich board laminate: Polymer A skin, Polymer B core, Polymer C adhesive.
  • Recycling route: shredding → controlled compression moulding (temperature/pressure/time).
  • Analyses: rheological, structural (optical microscopy), thermal (Tg, degradation), and mechanical (modulus/strength).
  • Design of Experiments: BBD varying temperature, pressure, and time; regression to a quadratic response surface.

Key Results

  • Thermal parity: recycled samples maintain glass transition trends similar to pristine material; degradation near ~410 °C.
  • Optimisation: response surface predicts flexural strength maximised at a specific combination of temperature, pressure and time.
  • Mechanical performance: recycled panels achieved meaningful stiffness/strength with process tuning.

Problem

Polycarbonate Glass Fiber (PCGF) composites present significant recycling challenges due to their complex material composition and limited end-of-life processing options. This research addresses the urgent need for sustainable recycling methods for PCGF materials in industrial applications.

Environmental Impact

  • • Limited recycling infrastructure
  • • High landfill waste volume
  • • Resource depletion concerns
  • • Carbon footprint reduction needs

Technical Challenges

  • • Material separation complexity
  • • Quality degradation during processing
  • • Cost-effective processing methods
  • • End-product market development

Approach

1

Material Analysis

Conducted comprehensive characterization of PCGF materials including thermal, mechanical, and chemical properties.

2

Processing Methods

Developed and tested multiple recycling processes including mechanical, thermal, and chemical treatment methods.

3

Quality Assessment

Evaluated recycled material properties and compared performance against virgin materials.

4

Economic Analysis

Assessed cost-effectiveness and market viability of proposed recycling solutions.
