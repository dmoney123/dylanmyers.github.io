Airplane Gearbox Design

Fully designed an in-depth model gearbox for a small airplane, given life and loading parameters using engineering design optimization principles.

The Problem

Design a fully-functional gearbox for a small airplane. Worked with McGill peers as a final project for engineering design course.

  • Weight constraints: Gearbox must be lightweight
  • Safety requirements: Must withstand extreme loads and fatigue cycles without failure
  • Efficiency optimization: Minimize power losses
  • Manufacturing feasibility: Design must be reasonable to produce

We needed to design a complete gearbox system that would meet all performance criteria.

The Approach

We approached this design challenge using systematic engineering methodology, combining theoretical analysis with CAD modeling to optimize the gearbox design. Design includes shafts, gears and bearings.

Design Process

  • • Load analysis and gear ratio calculations
  • • Material selection for strength and weight
  • • Iterative design approach
  • • Python beam analysis

Design Features

Iterative Stress-Life Analysis
3D Modeling
Weight Optimization

The Results

The completed gearbox design exceeded all performance requirements while minimizing weight:

Performance

  • • 15,000rpm output speed
  • • 1050 hp power
  • • Safety factor 1.5 - aviation standard
  • • 30,000+ hour life - exceeds requirements

Technical

  • • Detailed design
  • • Stress-Fatigue validation of all critical components
  • • Optimized design for weight
